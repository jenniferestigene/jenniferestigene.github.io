Chladni Patterns

Simulating the physics of vibrating plates through particle-based visualization

Tools p5.js, JavaScript Type Physics Simulation, Generative Art Particles 5,000 autonomous agents

Overview

Chladni patterns are the intricate formations created when a vibrating plate covered in sand or particles reveals the nodal lines where the plate doesn't move. This simulation recreates these fascinating patterns using particle agents that seek areas of minimal vibration, forming geometric designs that echo the mathematics of standing waves and resonance.

Technical Implementation

  • Framework: p5.js (JavaScript graphics library)
  • Particle System: 5,000 autonomous agents with steering behaviors
  • Chladni Equation: cos(n·π·x/L)·cos(m·π·y/L) - cos(m·π·x/L)·cos(n·π·y/L)
  • Pattern Variables: m and n determine the vibrational modes (1-6 range)
  • Seeking Behavior: Particles gravitate toward nodal lines (|value| > threshold)
  • Movement: Steering forces with max speed (2) and max force (0.1)
  • Boundary Handling: Edge wrapping within defined margins
  • Pattern Generation: Random m and n values ensure m ≠ n
  • Interaction: Click anywhere to generate a new random pattern
  • Source: Patt Vira
