Where Water Insecurity is Growing and Shrinking

Analyzing changes in household access to complete plumbing across U.S. states between 2022 and 2023

Date January 2025 Tools R, ggplot2, tidyverse Source U.S. Census Bureau (tidycensus) Type Dumbbell Chart, Comparative Analysis

Key Findings

This analysis examines the 20 states with the most significant changes in water insecurity between 2022 and 2023. The results reveal a split picture: 10 states saw conditions worsen while 10 states improved.

Alaska Increase

Alaska experienced the most dramatic increase, with the percentage of households lacking complete plumbing nearly doubling from 0.56% to 0.74%.

Context & Implications

Access to complete plumbing (defined as hot and cold running water, a flush toilet, and a bathtub or shower) is a fundamental measure of household infrastructure and public health capacity.

The data reveals troubling trends in states like Alaska, Utah, and Arizona, where water insecurity increased substantially. These states face unique challenges including remote geography, historically underserved indigenous communities, and climate pressures.

Conversely, states showing improvement demonstrate that progress is possible through targeted investment in rural infrastructure and community development programs.

Methodology

  • Data Source: U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey
  • Years: 2022 and 2023
  • Metric: Percentage of households lacking complete plumbing
  • Selection: Top 10 increases and top 10 decreases
  • Visualization: Dumbbell chart showing directional change
  • Processing: County data aggregated to state level, population-weighted
